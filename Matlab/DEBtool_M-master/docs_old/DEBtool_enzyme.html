Toolbox: enzyme

Compares enzyme performance in reactions between two complementary compounds

enzyme

Demo that runs:

  • pars_enzyme: parameter setting
  • shcontsu: countours for rates
  • shbatch: compouds in batch reactors
The models account for the enzyme-mediated transformation 1A + 1B -> 1C, using
1 standard enzyme kinetics: reversible binding of substrates to enzyme
2 special case: Synthezing Unit (irreversible binding)
3 special case: Rejection Unit (association as well as dissociation rates very large)

The enzyme can bind two substrates in a reversible way, without producing a product, but if both substrates are bound, it can produce a product. We assume that the product concentration does not affect the transformation rate.

For the more general transformation nA A + nB B -> nC C, the number of parameters of the transformation becomes excessive; there is a need for simplification.

A popular symplification is to let the assocation and dissociation rates between substrates and enzyme go to infinity, such that their ratios remain bounded. This results in a product of two hyperbolic terms for the production rate: the Rejection Unit (RU). This representation suffers from several theoretical problems.

An attractive alternative is to let the dissociation rates between substrates and enzyme go to zero: the Synthesizing Unit (SU). It allows a reformulation of the process in terms of fluxes, that can be applied in spatially structured environments (such as the living cell), where the concept `concentration' becomes problematic. It also allows a fully stochastic approach for describing the transformation. The DEB theory uses the SU as a module at several places.

The theory behind SU kinetics is given in:
Kooijman, S. A. L. M. 1998. The Synthesizing Unit as model for the stoichiometric fusion and branching of metabolic fluxes. Biophys. Chem. 73: 179 - 188

su11, ru11, enz11

The command [J_C, fractions] = su11(2.5, 3.1) assigns the flux of product to J_C and the fractions of bounded enzyme (.., A., .B, AB; the dot standing for free) to fractions, for concentration of substrate A = 2.5, and that of B = 3.1. This is the SU-case, ru11 and enz11 work in a similar way for the RU and the reversible enzyme case.

su

The command J_C = su(2.5, 3.1, 2, 3) assigns the flux of product to J_C, for concentration of substrate A = 2.5, and that of B = 3.1, given the reaction 2 A + 3 B -> C.
